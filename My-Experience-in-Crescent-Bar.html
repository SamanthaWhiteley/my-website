My Experience in Crescent Bar

The Beach

Going to the beach was one of my favorite activities while in Crescent Bar. My grandparent's trailer was only a few hundred feet from the beach, so some days would be spent almost entirely at the beach. There were also other beaches nearby that we frequently visited. My seconf cousin Kelsey and her parents also had a trailer at Crescent Bar, so we would go to the beach that was close to her house too. That beach had a rope swing attached to one of the trees that hung over the water, so we had a ton of fun swinging out into the water from the rope swing.

The Pool

Staying at the resort in the trailer gave us access to the community pool at Crescent Bar. This was another place that we frequently went while staying at Crescent Bar. The pool at Crescent Bar holds a lot of memories for me, as it was the pool I learned to swim in. My family would spend hours at the pool, playing games, and relaxing on the lounge chairs on the poolside.

Biking and Scootering

The trailer park at Crescent Bar had almost a grid of roads, while staying there we loved to ride our bikes and scooters through the streets. Crescent Bar is where I learned how to ride a bike. My sister and my favorite place to ride bikes or scooters was a smaller loop of the resort called south park, this was because the roads here had been most recently paved and the smooth ground was nice for riding.

The Fourth of July

Every summmer my family would be at Crescent Bar during the Fourth of July. There was a parade and firework show every year. A couple of times I was in the parade, but in years I wasn't we would always bring our camp chairs and watch the parade go by. But my favorite part of the holiday was the firework show. Everyone would bring camp chairs or blankets out to the golf course and watch the firework show.

The Trailer

Although we would spend most of our time in Crescent Bar outside, I loved my grandparent's trailer too. The trailer had a large deck that we would often eat dinner on, we would also play board games and card games into the night on the deck. The trailer also had a large yard that my family would play many games on. From croquet and volleyball, to running through the sprinklers and soaking up the sun. Often times when staying in Crescent Bar it wasn't just my immediate family, but my aunts, uncles, and cousins would be there too. When the whole family was there we would bring our Rv and park in the driveway, and the kids would set up tents in the yard so that we would have space for everyone.

The Shops

Another thing that became a tradition for us duirng each trip to Crescent Bar was going to the different shops and restaurants. There was an ice cream parlor called island cup and cone, my sister and would get the flavor cotton candy everytime. We also made it a tradition to go to the pizz parlor, Tower Pizza once per trip. Some of the other shops that we went to included the corner store, and the swimsuit shop.

Boating

One of my favorite aspects of going to Crescent Bar was boating. When we went boating we would ride out to a beach called "the dunes" because it was a small beach with large sand dunes. This was a very popular spot for people with boats or jetskis to go. When at the dunes my siblings and I would race to see who could make it up to the top of the dunes first. This was a challenge because the sand was so hot it would start to burn your feet before you could get to the top. Often times my family would pack lunch and enjoy the day at the dunes. We would play water games, go skiing, inner tubing, and wake boarding.
